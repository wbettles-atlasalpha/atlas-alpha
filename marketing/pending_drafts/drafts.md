## Snippet A: The Retail Trap
Most market participants focus on entry timing. They ask: 'Is this the bottom?' This approach is reactive and relies on personal price targets rather than objective market conditions. Institutional systems work in reverse. They start with the Structural Invalidation Thesis. They do not ask if the asset is a good buy. They ask at what data point the institutional logic for the current market move fails. By moving from 'price prediction' to 'data validation', you remove the emotional latency that consistently traps retail capital.

## Snippet C: The Concept of Data Decay
Market conditions change rapidly. A volume anomaly that was relevant yesterday may be noise today. Institutional algorithms operate on the assumption that Alpha conditions possess a limited half-life. Once the catalyst is priced in or the liquidity is absorbed, the edge disappears. Retail investors often fall into the trap of applying old logic to new market states. You must act when the data changes, not when your emotional tolerance is finally exceeded.
